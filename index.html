History of MacRitchie Reservoir

Situated off Lornie Road and completed in the late 1860s, MacRitchie Reservoir was the first water supply system implemented in Singapore. The Impounding Reservoir or Thomson Road Reservoir, as it was originally called, came about through the donation of S$13,000 by philanthropist Tan Kim Seng in 1857. The reservoir was renamed MacRitchie Reservoir in 1922 after James MacRitchie, who was Municipal Engineer of Singapore from 1883 to 1895.

Activity ideas for your trip to MacRitchie Reservoir!

1. Bird Watching - The reservoir is a bird sanctuary, allowing you to see a wide variety of bird species.
2. Tree Top Walking
3. Hiking - With different routes (3.2km & 4.8km) to challenge yourself!
4. Water Sports - Paddles, kayaks and canoes available for rent with specially designated area for beginners and another for experienced paddles.

*Things to take note of when visiting MacRitchie Reservoir*

1. Always be aware of your surroundings and look out for macaques. Avoid holding food and plastic bags on your hands as they might mistake it for food!
2. In case of lightning alert, always head to the nearest shelter away from trees to avoid the dangers of a category 1 storm.
3. Always bring a bottle of water along with you while going on long walks and hikes. Stay hydrated!
4. Should there be any suspicious person or article loitering or lying around, please call 999.
